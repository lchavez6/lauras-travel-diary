Laura's Travel Diary

After having moved to America when I was 5 years old, I realized that traveling would always be an important part of my life. As I got older, I looked forward to the plane rides and imagined my life as an adult when I would be able to choose any destination I desired. I knew I didn’t want to travel alone, but at 22, I finally got the chance to travel alone, and it was an experience that changed my life. I’d done a lot more traveling before turning 22, but it was always with a partner. I didn’t think I had anything to prove to myself, but this solo trip ended up being my favorite because it is where I learned to enjoy my own company.
I have created this travel diary to document some of my favorite travel destinations and memories. Traveling has shaped who I am, and I would like to share this with other people so that they can be inspired to travel alone or go to a destination that perhaps wasn’t too appealing beforehand.

Sao Paulo, Brazil (2014)

My best friend from high school had moved back to Brazil during our freshman year and I missed her a lot. I didn’t have many friends growing up, so when my mom asked me what I wanted for my graduation present, I didn’t hesitate. I wanted a trip to visit my best friend. I planned this trip for a major part of my senior year, and I would often look at pictures of what the country looked like. I thought maybe it couldn’t be much different than Colombia except for the different language, and I feel like I was somewhat right.
I took a United flight sometime in June, and I remember it took almost 10 hours to get there on a non-stop flight! During the flight, they showed pictures of the many different parts of Brazil and although I knew I wouldn’t be leaving the capital, I kept imagining that I would. Some of the parts that most intrigued me were Rio de Janeiro, especially Ipanema. It’s interesting how things are connected because later in college I took a music class, and I heard this song called “Girl from Ipanema” and I felt so worldly! Looking back, I wish I could have explored more on my own, but I was too scared back then. I want to go back to Brazil and go see the Iguazu waterfalls. My grandparents visited them during their honeymoon, and I want to see them too. Anyways, getting back to Brazil.
I landed Guarulhos International Airport, and it was much more humid than I could have imagined. My friend picked me up on a big bus, which was a new experience for me. After getting back to her house, we went out for lunch and although I don’t remember what we ate, I do remember we enjoyed many Guaranas which is a ginger ale like beverage except 100 times better!
I was a little disappointed that Sao Paulo was like a big metropolis, but I do remember admiring the high-rise buildings. During my time here, my friend took me to Sushi and told me that there a big Japanese population in this city.

One of my favorite movies is Lost in Translation and the inside of the Sushi restaurant reminded me of this. Prior to eating here, I thought Sushi was just a California roll and boy, was I wrong!

My trip was short, and my friend felt bad that we couldn’t go to too many places while I was there, so she planned a trip with some of her other friends to a Cunha. It was a little bit far away by car ride and I was never huge into hiking or going camping, but my friend said I would like it, so I went. It was funny because the World Cup was taking place during time, and we didn’t even get tickets! While I was in Cunha though, I watched some of the games, and even cried a little bit when James Rodriguez cried as he lost to Brazil. Colombia was so close to winning!

The exterior of the house where we stayed in Cunha. This mini getaway felt very homely and although I feared being so far from home, I felt very excited and independent.

Mexico, 2016

After I got back from Brazil that summer, I started going to school at George Mason in August. I didn’t travel much during the two years I spent at Mason, but this all changed when I moved to Tampa, Florida in 2016. I loved living in Tampa because there was so much to do. My favorite place to visit, besides the beach, was Ginnie Springs! Anyways, one day I was searching to see what flights to some places I wanted to visit cost, and I was pleasantly surprised that you could go just about anywhere, for cheap, out of the Orlando airport.
I continued my undergraduate degree at The University of South Florida. However, I had to wait one year to receive in state tuition. While I was waiting, I planned two trips. One to Cancun, Mexico and the other to Europe. I was a woman on a mission! I spent three days in Cancun and about a month after coming back, I convinced my then boyfriend to visit 5 European countries with me in 11 days. We’d bought new sneakers before our trip, and the soles were completely worn by the time we returned from Europe.

Looking back at my pictures, I’m sad to find that I don’t have many pictures from my Mexico trip. The beach was so beautiful! But I do have some from the tourist dolphin swim and the Cenote’s tour I did as well.

The dolphin’s skin felt so leathery!

Europe, Summer 2017

Europe was always a dream of mine. I’ve always wanted to see the Eiffel tower, the colosseum, and the various museums throughout. I knew that planning to visit so many countries in such a short period was a big undertaking, but I was ready. I planned the itinerary for months and everything went quite smoothly, besides not being able to see the Anne Frank house. One of my favorite artists is Van Gogh so I really loved going to see his artwork in France. For this trip, I flew out of MCO (Orlando) and had a layover in Manchester. After the layover, I landed in Amsterdam, and it was like something out of a movie. The air smelled so clean, and although it was still a little cold and a grey, I managed to have a wonderful time. The first night there I remember that we were outside walking around 11pm at night and there was still sunlight! The highlights of Amsterdam were Rembrandt Square, the coffee houses, The Heineken Experience, Madame Tussaud’s wax museum, The Lookout (Europe’s highest swing at the time), and the Rijksmuseum.

We were so high up! Also, RIP sneakers.

We took a canal cruise, and it was so much fun!

I loved the look of the houses in Amsterdam, they were actually very similar to the houses in Copenhagen, Denmark.

We quickly changed scenery after a couple of days and landed in Rome. What I enjoyed most about Rome aside from the weather, was how welcoming the people were. It reminded me a lot of Colombia. I also found their cuisine exquisite and appreciated that we could drink a carafe of wine for 5 euros. While here, the highlights were being able to visit the Vatican City and see its museums, where Michelangelo totally blew us away. I also loved seeing the statues and the Galleria Borghese. The artists I admired most were Caravaggio and Correggio.

What was most impressive about the sculptures were the details. I remember on one of them, you could see the imprint of the man’s fingers on the woman’s thighs, and it was stunning to know that this artwork had survived so many years.
One of my favorite art pieces was the Pieta, which is the virgin Mary carrying the body of Christ.

We weren’t supposed to take pictures of the Sistine Chapel but we managed to sneak a camera in!

This is the Pieta.

After eating our way throughout Rome, we soon left and landed in Paris. It was a late flight and I felt so happy to be greeted by the shining Eiffel tower. The first time I saw it light up, I cried because I felt successful in making my dreams come true. The food was also really good in Paris, and my favorite were the escargot. Some of the highlights from this city were the Catacombs, the Louvre Museum, the Palace of Versailles, Notre Dame, the cruise on the Siene, and being able to go to the top of the Eiffel tower where we enjoyed glasses of champagne.

The Mona Lisa was much smaller and less impressive in person.

After leaving Paris, we landed in Copenhagen. We didn’t have much planned for this city, except seeing the Little Mermaid. We also wanted to take the Oresund bridge to cross over into Sweden, and the train because the tunnel went underwater. I can’t remember much about Sweden aside from its grey atmosphere, and Copenhagen wasn’t too impressive either aside from the famous Nyhvan neighborhood. The houses were so quaint!

Malmo, Sweden.

Tropical Destinations

After being away for so long during the Europe trip, I wanted to take a few more short trips with beach and relaxation involved. When I turned 21, I bought tickets to go to Montego Bay and I stayed at Sunset at The Palms. The beach there was so calm. Everyone was friendly and the food was delicious. I remember jumping off the cliffs in the ocean, snorkeling, going to Rick’s café, and spending a day on the party boat/catamaran. This vacation though, was very similar to a trip I planned two years later to Punta Cana in the DR which is why I have grouped these together. The main difference between these trips is that the heat/ sun in DR was much hotter and intense. Also, the people in DR were all about the party and drinking, whereas the Jamaicans were much more laid back. I recently took a trip to Puerto Rico to celebrate my graduation. I think at this point I have concluded that most tropical places are the same. If you’ve seen one, you’ve seen them all. Doesn’t mean they’re not fun though!

China, 2019.

A little out of chronological order, but I have included my China trip last because it was the most freeing. I had to travel to Los Angeles to catch a flight into Beijing in November of 2019 over Thanksgiving weekend. I spent a few days in LA surfing and seeing some landmarks. I also went on a mini cruise and got to see many of the seals in Long Beach before I made my way over to China. The flight was nonstop, but it was almost sixteen hours! When I landed in Beijing, I got some lunch and was surprised to know that they had a knock off version of red bull in small weirdly shaped cans for a couple of Yen. My biggest surprise upon landing was that they had squatting toilets. I never used one, I was too intimidated. The trip to China was very long as well. It was almost 11 days where I got to go all around China. This was also my first time traveling alone. I was scared but ended up having a great time. The highlights of my trip were seeing the Shanghai Skyline, climbing The Great Wall in Beijing, going to the Forbidden City, and seeing the Chinese Opera. I wasn’t too impressed with traditional Chinese food but loved the duck and the dim sum.
