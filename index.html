Laura's Travel Diary

After having moved to America when I was 5 years old, I realized that traveling would always be an important part of my life. As I got older, I looked forward to the plane rides and imagined my life as an adult when I would be able to choose any destination I desired. I knew I didn’t want to travel alone, but at 22, I finally got the chance to travel alone, and it was an experience that changed my life. I’d done a lot more traveling before turning 22, but it was always with a partner. I didn’t think I had anything to prove to myself, but this solo trip ended up being my favorite because it is where I learned to enjoy my own company.
I have created this travel diary to document some of my favorite travel destinations and memories. Traveling has shaped who I am, and I would like to share this with other people so that they can be inspired to travel alone or go to a destination that perhaps wasn’t too appealing beforehand.

Sao Paulo, Brazil (2014)

My best friend from high school had moved back to Brazil during our freshman year and I missed her a lot. I didn’t have many friends growing up, so when my mom asked me what I wanted for my graduation present, I didn’t hesitate. I wanted a trip to visit my best friend. I planned this trip for a major part of my senior year, and I would often look at pictures of what the country looked like. I thought maybe it couldn’t be much different than Colombia except for the different language, and I feel like I was somewhat right.
I took a United flight sometime in June, and I remember it took almost 10 hours to get there on a non-stop flight! During the flight, they showed pictures of the many different parts of Brazil and although I knew I wouldn’t be leaving the capital, I kept imagining that I would. Some of the parts that most intrigued me were Rio de Janeiro, especially Ipanema. It’s interesting how things are connected because later in college I took a music class, and I heard this song called “Girl from Ipanema” and I felt so worldly! Looking back, I wish I could have explored more on my own, but I was too scared back then. I want to go back to Brazil and go see the Iguazu waterfalls. My grandparents visited them during their honeymoon, and I want to see them too. Anyways, getting back to Brazil.
I landed Guarulhos International Airport, and it was much more humid than I could have imagined. My friend picked me up on a big bus, which was a new experience for me. After getting back to her house, we went out for lunch and although I don’t remember what we ate, I do remember we enjoyed many Guaranas which is a ginger ale like beverage except 100 times better!
I was a little disappointed that Sao Paulo was like a big metropolis, but I do remember admiring the high-rise buildings. During my time here, my friend took me to Sushi and told me that there a big Japanese population in this city.

One of my favorite movies is Lost in Translation and the inside of the Sushi restaurant reminded me of this. Prior to eating here, I thought Sushi was just a California roll and boy, was I wrong!

My trip was short, and my friend felt bad that we couldn’t go to too many places while I was there, so she planned a trip with some of her other friends to a Cunha. It was a little bit far away by car ride and I was never huge into hiking or going camping, but my friend said I would like it, so I went. It was funny because the World Cup was taking place during time, and we didn’t even get tickets! While I was in Cunha though, I watched some of the games, and even cried a little bit when James Rodriguez cried as he lost to Brazil. Colombia was so close to winning!

The exterior of the house where we stayed in Cunha. This mini getaway felt very homely and although I feared being so far from home, I felt very excited and independent.

Finally, a Brazilian cookout!

One of the most beautiful sunsets I have ever seen!

The dreaded hike. Very strenuous but completely worth it.

My friend’s parents loved to travel a lot and I remember seeing this on their fridge. I hope I can have a fridge as full as this one, one day.
